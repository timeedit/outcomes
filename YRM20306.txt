["YRM20306","Research Methodology in Environmental Science","1MO, 2MO","After successful completion of this course students are expected to be able to:- explain the difference between a conceptual and a technical research design;- describe the defining features of an experimental, cross-sectional, and longitudinal design;- discuss the operationalization of one- and multidimensional concepts;- understand reasons and strategies for random and non-random sampling;- describe the pros and cons of the taught methods of data collection;- understand the conditions that must be met for causal claims;- discuss the reliability and validity of measurements;- contribute to interdisciplinary research designs for the environmental sciences;- critically read scientific literature.",""]
