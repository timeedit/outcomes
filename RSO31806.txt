["RSO31806","Sociology of Food and Place","5MO","After successful completion of this course students are expected to be able to: - understand sociological, anthropological and geographical debates about food provisioning and place-based development processes and their changing social and political context;- analyse the multi-actor, multi-level and multi-aspect dynamics of food provisioning and place-based development; - evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of different theoretical concepts and perspectives used to analyse the complexity and dynamics of food provisioning and place-based development; - operationalize theoretical concepts and perspectives by applying them in the design of research; - identify appropriate methods for conducting socio-spatial research on food and place; - reflect upon their role as a social scientist;- draft a research proposal on one of the topics of or related to the course.",""]
