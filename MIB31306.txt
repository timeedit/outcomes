["MIB31306","Microbial Ecology","1AF","After successful completion of this course students are expected to be able to: - describe, classify & examine different types of interactions between microorganisms and their biological and physico-chemical environment; - compare the role of microorganisms in the cycling of elements such as carbon, nitrogen and sulphur; - explain how microorganisms can transform environmental pollutants; - examine experimental observations as indicators of specific microbial processes; - apply modern molecular techniques for the analysis of complex microbial ecosystems; - predict ecological functions from genomic and metagenomic data; - design experimental strategies for the detection of microbes and their activities in the environment.",""]
