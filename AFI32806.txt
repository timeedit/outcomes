["AFI32806","Marine Resources Management","3WD","After successful completion of this course students are expected to be able to:- apply the concept of the management cycle (a continuous decision-making process based on uncertain information) to problems arising from the use of marine and freshwater living resources - explain the main economic and ecological concepts and tools used in management of marine and freshwater living resources, and correctly apply them to a real case; - apply the social-ecological system framework to analyse problems around the use of marine and freshwater living resources;- analyse the trade-offs between conflicting objectives in marine resources management;- identify strategies and measures to reconcile conflicting objectives;- evaluate the effectiveness of measures taken to change and adapt marine resource use and management;- assess a specific marine or freshwater resource use management problem with the tools provided and design, present and defend a potential solution.",""]
